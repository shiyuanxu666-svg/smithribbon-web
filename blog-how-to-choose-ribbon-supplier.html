1. Verify the Supplier is a Real Factory

The single most common mistake is confusing a trading company with a true manufacturer. Trading companies mark up prices by 20-40% and have no control over production quality.

How to verify a real ribbon factory:

  • Request a live video tour of the production floor — not pre-recorded photos
  • Ask for footage showing the weaving machines in operation
  • Verify the business license mentions "manufacturing" (制造业)
  • Check employee count — a real factory has 50+ workers minimum
  • Ask for SMETA or BSCI audit reports (third-party verified)
⚠️ Red Flag: A supplier that refuses video calls, only sends stock photos, or has a website with no factory address is likely a trading agent. Smith Ribbon welcomes video factory tours — 15,000㎡ production base in Xiamen, China.

3. Must-Have Certifications for International Buyers

Different markets require different certifications. Here's what to look for:

  • OEKO-TEX Standard 100 — Required for all textiles sold in EU and USA. Tests for harmful substances.
  • ISO 9001 — Quality management system certification. Ensures consistent production standards.
  • BSCI / SEDEX / SMETA — Social compliance audits. Required by major retailers (Walmart, Target, Tesco) to verify worker conditions.
  • FSC Certification — Required for paper-based ribbons and packaging materials to prove sustainable sourcing.

5. Pricing Red Flags to Watch For

  • Price too low to be real: If the quoted price is 30% below market average, the supplier is cutting corners — typically using thinner materials or inferior dyes.
  • Hidden setup fees: Some suppliers advertise low per-meter prices but charge $200-500 for printing plates, molds, or color matching.
  • Vague payment terms: Legitimate factories ask for 30-50% deposit with balance before shipping. Be wary of suppliers demanding 100% prepayment.
  • No quality inspection report: Reputable factories conduct final inspection before shipment and provide a QC report.
